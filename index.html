Hello ^_^
My name is Sarah. I use the name akibaten in most places.
I love being creative, but I really struggle to create full, cohesive, completed works. This site is my attempt to combat that. I create a lot (or at least I try to), and for so long, everything I made has been more or less ephemeral. Poetry lost in journals or songs played once in a dj set and never again or drawings stashed in forgotten sketchbooks. I think a part of me is scared of being judged or scared of being looked at. On this site I can kind of just put anything I want without constantly judging myself (or less so than I would otherwise). When I do this, the site and my art kind of grow with me in a big cohesive project that's really just me. So when I am proud of something, instead of waiting because I want it to be a part of some huge project that might not materialize, I can just put it here.

Favorite pieces of media right now:

Inside Mari by Shuzo Oshimi

Inside Mari is a manga about a really existentially lost guy that one day wakes up in the body of a girl. It begins as a mystery as he searches for why this happened, but quickly becomes much more complicated and psychological. I can't say anymore without spoiling stuff so you have to read it ;) It helped me figure out who I was and taught me a lot about identity not just as an abstract concept but a lens through which we see the world and understand ourselves, and as cringe as it feels to say, it kind of... healed a part of me.

The End of Evangelion

The End of Evangelion is my favorite movie of all time. It's so powerful, and when I watched it for the first time I felt every idea and concept it showed interface with my soul in a way thats hard to describe. It is the perfect end to any piece of media I've ever watched and does an incredible job of exploring idas about spirituality and the concept of a system/outcome/chain of events being perfectly imperfect.

All About Lily Chou chou

Another movie I love to death. Its a heart-wrenching watch, but it portrays so much and says so much. In writing this right now I realize sometimes its okay to not abstract some thing we love so we can explain why we like it. I like this movie because I can feel it. The imagery, emotions, writing... it all hits my brain in such a beautiful way and tumbles down all the paths of my feelings in such a unique way I haven't felt it anywhere else.

The Importance of Existence

Existence is so weird and confusing, and infinitely complex for every person, and I feel like if I struggle to make big projects or albums, maybe that's because I should try to make my whole existence a project in it of itself. I'm not sure there could be a project that could be bigger than that, or at least not one I can make by my lonesome.

I've always tried to push myself to never have a filter about what I do and don't talk about with relation to myself. I think recently I have started to internalize that it is okay for me to have stuff in my brain just for me, but even still those things are pretty rare.

This website is about as me as I can possibly be publicly. We all have so much life we need to live, and I hope people perusing my existence will make someone as happy as perusing other people's inner worlds makes me.
